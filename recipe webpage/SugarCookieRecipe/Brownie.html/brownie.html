Triple Chocolate Brownie

Satisfy your sweet tooth with this top-rated sugar cookie recipe. It comes together with just a few ingredients you likely already have on hand — and the sugar cookies turn out perfect every time.

Why you'll love these sugar cookies

  • Soft, thick centers with slightly crisp edges
  • Irresistible buttery vanilla flavor
  • Leave plain or flavor with extras like maple, cinnamon, and more
  • Flat surface for decorating
  • Stay soft for days
  • Colorful designs

Why you'll love these sugar cookies

  • preparation time: 20-40 mins
  • chilling time:1-2 hours
  • baking time: 7-13 mins
  • total time:2hr-3hrs

Cookie ingredients

  • All-Purpose Flour: The base of the cookie.
  • Unsalted Butter: For richness and texture, softened.
  • Granulated Sugar: For sweetness, plus more for rolling.
  • Eggs: Bind ingredients and add richness (sometimes an extra yolk for chewiness).
  • Vanilla Extract: Essential for flavor.
  • Baking Powder: A leavening agent for a bit of lift.
  • Baking Soda: Also helps with leavening and spread.
  • Salt: Balances the sweetness.

Icing ingredients

  • Powdered Sugar (Confectioners' Sugar): The base for sweetness and structure.
  • Liquid (Milk/Water): Thins the icing to the desired consistency (outline vs. flood).
  • Corn Syrup (Light): Adds shine and helps the icing dry firm and stackable.
  • Vanilla or Almond Extract: Provides flavor; clear extract keeps colors pure.
  • Food Coloring (Gel): For vibrant colors without adding too much liquid.

Sugar Cookie Procedure

  1. Combine the dry ingredients (flour, salt, and baking powder) in a bowl and set aside.
  2. Cream the softened butter and granulated sugar together until light and fluffy.
  3. Add the eggs one at a time, followed by the vanilla extract, mixing until just combined.
  4. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing on low speed until a dough forms. Avoid overmixing.
  5. Divide the dough into two disks, wrap in plastic, and chill for at least 2 hours. Chilling helps the cookies maintain their shape during baking.
  6. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  7. Roll out the chilled dough to about ¼-inch thickness on a lightly floured surface. Cut out shapes using cookie cutters and place them on the prepared baking sheets.
  8. Bake for 8 to 12 minutes until the edges are lightly browned. Do not overbake for soft cookies.
  9. Let the cookies cool on the baking sheet for a few minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely. Decorate once cooled.

Icing Procedure

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together powdered sugar and a small amount of milk until smooth and thick.
  2. Mix in vanilla extract (or almond extract for a classic bakery taste).
  3. Divide into bowls and add food coloring, mixing well until the desired shades are achieved.
  4. Use a piping bag or squeeze bottle to trace the edges of each cooled cookie.
  5. Spoon or pipe the thinner icing inside the outline and gently spread with a toothpick.
  6. While the icing is wet, add sprinkles or designs. For layered designs, let the base dry first.
  7. Allow cookies to dry at room temperature for 1–2 hours (or overnight) until the icing is firm and glossy.
